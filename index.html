Co-belligerents

The Central Powers were supported by various co-belligerents during the war...

  • South African Republic: In opposition to Union of South Africa's operations, Germany assisted Boer army officers.
  • Senussi Order: A Muslim political-religious tariqa in Libya, courted by the Ottoman Empire and Germany.
  • Sultanate of Darfur: Allied with the Ottomans in 1915 against the Anglo-Egyptian forces.
  • Zaian Confederation: Fought against France to prevent French expansion into Morocco.
  • Dervish State: Fought against British, Ethiopian, Italian, and French Empires with support from Germany and Ottoman Empire.

Client States

The Central Powers had several client states...

  • Poland: The Kingdom of Poland was a client state of Germany proclaimed in 1916.
  • Lithuania: The Kingdom of Lithuania was created as a client state of Germany in 1918.
  • Belarus: The Belarusian Democratic Republic was established in 1918.
  • Ukraine: The Ukrainian State was led by Hetman Pavlo Skoropadskyi in 1918.
  • Crimea: The Crimean Regional Government was a client state of Germany created in 1918.
  • Courland and Semigallia: The Duchy was established as a client state of Germany in 1918.
  • Baltic State: Also known as the "United Baltic Duchy", it was proclaimed by the Baltic German ruling class.
  • Finland: Supported by Germany after gaining independence from Russia in 1917.
  • Georgia: Declared independence in 1918 with German support.
  • Don: The Don Republic founded in 1918 with pro-German sympathies.
  • Jabal Shammar: An Arab state closely associated with the Ottoman Empire.
  • Azerbaijan: The Azerbaijan Democratic Republic supported by the Ottoman Empire in 1918.
  • Qatar: Initially an Ottoman puppet, later a British puppet.
  • Yemen: An autonomous region of the Ottoman Empire that fought against the Allies.

Nations Supported by Central Powers

These nations cooperated with the Central Powers, making their neutrality disputable...

  • Ethiopia: Suspected of sympathy for the Central Powers, provided indirect support.
  • Liechtenstein: Officially neutral but supportive of Central Powers, faced economic struggles due to embargoes.
  • Upper Asir: Fought against Asir in 1916.
  • Kingdom of Greece: Supported by the Central Powers until 1917.
  • Romania: Fought alongside the Central Powers until rejoining the war against them in 1918.
